HUMANITY has made great strides in exploring the great mysteries of the cosmos. We have observed the afterglow of the big bang, spying on galaxies so far away that we see them as they were when the universe was a baby, tracking distant supernova explosions, and penetrating to the very edge of a black hole. However, one corner of the cosmos has proved resistant to our attempts to deal with it: the part we call home, the Milky Way.

With the Milky Way, we are inside what we are trying to observe. We can only dream of flying out of our galaxy in a spaceship to see a different perspective. Therefore, we must be content with our view from an insignificant blue spot orbiting one star out of many billions in a vast agglomeration of stellar neighborhoods.
